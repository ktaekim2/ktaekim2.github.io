  <h1>About This Blog</h1>

  <p>
    This blog is my public architecture decision log. It is not a tutorial site,
    and it is not meant to prove that I can write polished English from scratch.
    Its purpose is to show how I think through engineering problems: context,
    constraints, trade-offs, decisions, and the operational risks behind them.
  </p>

  <h2>What I Write About</h2>

  <p>
    I mainly write about backend engineering, Java/Spring systems, legacy
    migration, reliability, maintainability, observability, and practical
    architecture decisions from real work. Sensitive company details are removed
    or generalised before publication.
  </p>

  <h2>Use of AI</h2>

  <p>
    I use AI as an editor and structuring assistant. It helps me refine rough
    notes, improve English readability, and organise my thinking into a clearer
    ADR format.
  </p>

  <p>
    The engineering context, decisions, trade-offs, final judgement, and review
    are mine. If a post says I chose or rejected an option, that reflects my own
    reasoning, not an AI-generated conclusion.
  </p>

  <p>
    In short: AI helps polish the communication, but the engineering judgement is
    what I am trying to document here.
  </p>
